# ST Lab TTML Truth Report

## 1. Executive Summary

TTML is a W3C timed-text interchange language whose core Recommendation defines vocabulary and semantics for timed text documents, while profiles constrain the base language for particular delivery or workflow contexts ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, Abstract and §1](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)). TTML is intended for interchange and transcoding among subtitling and captioning formats, and may also be used directly as a distribution format referenced from HTML or SMIL contexts ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, Abstract](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

For ST Lab, the conservative model is to treat TTML validation as a combination of XML/schema checks, TTML abstract-document conformance checks, profile-resolution checks, timing-interval checks, style/layout semantic checks, and render-aware checks where a profile constrains presentation complexity or region behavior ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §§3.1, 4, 5.2.4, 10, 11, 12](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)). A schema-only validator is insufficient because TTML2 explicitly states that referenced schemas do not validate all syntactic constraints and represent a superset of conformant TTML Content ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §4.1](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

The primary stable baseline for generic TTML engineering should be TTML2 Recommendation 08 November 2018, with TTML1 Third Edition Recommendation 08 November 2018 retained for legacy compatibility, DFXP terminology, and historical profile names ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, front matter](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/); [W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §1](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)). IMSC 1.2 is the latest W3C IMSC Recommendation located by this run and defines both text-only and image-only delivery profiles, while IMSC Text Profile 1.3 and DAPT are Candidate Recommendation Drafts and should be modeled as draft/profile targets rather than stable Recommendation baselines unless a project explicitly selects them ([W3C IMSC 1.2, Recommendation, copyright 2020, Status and role text](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc/rec); [W3C IMSC Text Profile 1.3, Candidate Recommendation Draft 03 April 2026, front matter](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc1.3/); [W3C DAPT, Candidate Recommendation Draft 10 February 2026, Status](https://www.w3.org/TR/dapt/)).

Historical DFXP should be treated as lineage and profile terminology, not as a separate modern standard overriding TTML1 or TTML2, because TTML1 states that earlier drafts referred to TTML as DFXP and that the term is retained for historical reasons in profile names, designators, and older URLs ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §1](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)). Any ST Lab feature that imports “DFXP” should normalize it to TTML1/TTML profile identifiers and preserve the original label as source metadata rather than inferring a different conformance regime ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §1 and §5.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)).

## 4. TTML Timing and Structure Engineering Model

### Document hierarchy

TTML1 describes a document instance as a `tt` root containing header and body, where the header carries document-level metadata, styling definitions, and layout definitions, and the body carries text content plus references to style/layout information and inline styling/timing information ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §1.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)). TTML2 organizes the specification into abstract document types for TTML Content, TTML Intermediate, and TTML Profile documents, with `tt`, `isd:sequence`/`isd:isd`, and `ttp:profile` as the respective root element types ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §§4.1-4.3](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

The TTML1 content vocabulary includes `tt`, `head`, `body`, `div`, `p`, `span`, and `br`, and TTML1 defines `p` as a logical paragraph that transitions between block-level and inline-level formatting semantics ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §§7.1 and 7.1.5](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)). TTML2 extends the vocabulary model beyond TTML1 by including modules for animation, audio, content, data, document, font, head, image, layout, metadata, profile, and styling vocabulary ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §5.4.1](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

### Timing semantics

TTML2 timing is centered on the `begin`, `dur`, `end`, and `timeContainer` attributes, where `begin` establishes an included interval start, `end` establishes an excluded interval end, `dur` may be zero, and `timeContainer` determines whether timed children are parallel or sequential ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §§12.2.1-12.2.4](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)). TTML2’s active interval semantics require ST Lab to represent intervals as `[begin, end)`, because the presentation effects of a non-empty interval do not extend into the frame or tick equal to or immediately following the ending point ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §12.2.3](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

TTML2 time expressions can use clock-time, offset-time, or wallclock-time forms, and profile-specific rules may restrict which of those forms are permitted or require `ttp:frameRate` or `ttp:tickRate` when `f` or `t` metrics appear ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §12.3.1](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/); [W3C IMSC 1.2, Recommendation, copyright 2020, §§8.12.7 and 8.12.10](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc/rec)). TTML2 defines separate conversion semantics for clock, media, and SMPTE time bases, so ST Lab should store original time base, expression syntax, effective frame/tick parameters, and normalized media-time only when the standard and available inputs support that conversion ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, Appendices I.1-I.3](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

### Profile and processor semantics

TTML2 distinguishes content profiles from processor profiles, and multiple profiles may be combined into an effective content profile or effective processor profile using profile-combination semantics ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §§5.2.1 and 5.2.4](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)). A TTML2 effective validation profile is the effective content profile when non-null and otherwise the effective processor profile, so ST Lab validation should expose which profile was actually used for validation rather than reporting only the declared profile strings ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §5.3.1.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).

IMSC 1.2 defines Text and Image profile designators, and it explicitly states that a processor conforming to one profile is not required to conform to the other, so ST Lab should model IMSC Text and Image support as independent capabilities ([W3C IMSC 1.2, Recommendation, copyright 2020, conformance and profile-designator sections](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc/rec)). IMSC Text Profile 1.3 removes the Image Profile from that document and is explicitly text-only, so ST Lab should not treat IMSC 1.3 draft as a drop-in replacement for IMSC 1.2 Image workflows ([W3C IMSC Text Profile 1.3, Candidate Recommendation Draft 03 April 2026, §§2.1 and L.2.3](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc1.3/)).

## 5. Styling/Layout Constraints for Interchange

TTML styling is a separable layer that expresses authorial presentation intentions, and TTML1 describes style association methods including inline styling, referential styling, chained referential styling, and nested styling ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §§8 and 8.4.1](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)). TTML1 gives inline styling higher priority than other style association forms and states that chained style-reference loops must be considered errors ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §§8.4.1.1 and 8.4.1.3](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)).

TTML2 defines `region` as a rectangular space into which content is flowed, and TTML2 `region` references must point to `region` elements with a `layout` ancestor ([W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §§11.1.2 and 11.2.1](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)). TTML1 default-region semantics apply when no `region` element is specified, but if `region` elements exist then region association follows the ordered rules stated in TTML1 §9.3.3 ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §§9.3.1 and 9.3.3](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/)).

IMSC adds interchange constraints that are narrower than generic TTML, including the requirement that `tts:extent` on each `region` use two pixel, percentage, or root-container-relative length expressions, and the requirement that `ttp:frameRate` or `ttp:tickRate` be present when frame or tick time expressions are used ([W3C IMSC 1.2, Recommendation, copyright 2020, §§8.12.7, 8.12.8, 8.12.10, 9.5.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc/rec)). IMSC also defines a hypothetical render model that limits document complexity and treats painting failures before an intermediate synchronic document’s presentation time as errors, so ST Lab should treat IMSC render-complexity validation as render-aware rather than schema-only ([W3C IMSC 1.2, Recommendation, copyright 2020, Hypothetical Render Model section](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml-imsc/rec)).

Non-normative implementation guidance: ST Lab should compute and cache specified, computed, used, and actual values separately only where the selected validation/rendering path needs those phases, because TTML1 distinguishes those style value categories and TTML2 defines detailed style-resolution procedures ([W3C TTML1 Third Edition, Recommendation 08 November 2018, edited in place 20 August 2020, §§8.4.3-8.4.4](https://www.w3.org/TR/2018/REC-ttml1-20181108/); [W3C TTML2, Recommendation 08 November 2018, §10.4.4](https://www.w3.org/TR/ttml2/)).
