# ST Lab PTP Timing and Synchronization Truth Report

## Executive Summary

ST Lab should treat PTP timing as a separate engineering plane whose design data must be traceable to IEEE 1588, SMPTE ST 2059, ST 2110 timing dependencies, and RTP clock-signalling rules, because IEEE 1588-2019 defines Precision Time Protocol for precise clock synchronization in packet-based networked systems and SMPTE states that the ST 2059 suite defines media synchronization using PTP packets on an IT network ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html), [SMPTE Standards Quarterly Report December 2024](https://www.smpte.org/hubfs/SMPTE%20Standards%20Quarterly%20Report%20December%202024.pdf?hsLang=en)). The accessible official SMPTE pages for ST 2059-2:2021 and ST 2110-10:2022 identify the documents and active/latest status but do not expose clause-level timing requirements, so ST 2059 and ST 2110 timing conformance claims in ST Lab must remain Unverified unless a licensed clause source is attached ([SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2021), [SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022)).

The strongest public clause-level timing requirements available for ST Lab planning are the RTP timestamp requirements in RFC 3550 and the reference-clock/media-clock signalling requirements in RFC 7273 ([RFC 3550](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html), [RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)). RFC 3550 requires RTP timestamps to derive from a monotonically and linearly incrementing clock and states that direct comparison of RTP timestamps from different media streams is not effective for synchronization, because different media streams may advance at different rates and have independent random offsets ([RFC 3550 Section 5.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html)). RFC 7273 defines SDP signalling for timestamp reference clocks and media clocks, including `ts-refclk` and `mediaclk`, and requires local or asynchronous defaults when signalling is absent ([RFC 7273 Sections 4.8, 5.4, and 6](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).

Operationally, ST Lab should model grandmaster identity, PTP domain, profile identity, clock traceability, per-port role, path asymmetry assumptions, boundary/transparent clock presence, RTP clock-source signalling, and monitoring evidence as first-class fields, but this is non-normative design guidance derived from the cited standards and secondary broadcast guidance ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html), [RFC 7273 Section 4.3](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html), [SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip)). Secondary guidance reports ST 2059-2 operational values such as maximum offset less than 1 microsecond within 5 seconds and default message rates, but those values are treated here as secondary operational guidance, not as verified normative clauses, because the accessible SMPTE ST 2059-2 DOI page does not show the clauses ([WSTS/ATIS ST 2059-2 monitoring presentation](https://wsts.atis.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Monitoring-Analysis-of-SMPTE-ST-2059-2-PTP-Networks-Media-Devices.KERNEN.pdf), [SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2021)).

## Engineering Timing Model for ST Lab

### PTP architecture model

ST Lab should represent a timing architecture as a graph of timing domains, candidate time sources, grandmasters, intermediate clock-capable network devices, and endpoint clocks because IEEE 1588-2019 states that PTP enables synchronization of real-time clocks in networked distributed systems and defines timing domains, timing topology, profiles, management, and network mappings at the standard-description level ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html)). ST Lab should store the PTP profile in use as a distinct field because IEEE 1588-2019 states that customization is supported by profiles and SMPTE ST 2059-2 is identified by SMPTE as the profile for IEEE 1588 PTP in professional broadcast applications ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html), [SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2021)).

ST Lab should store grandmaster ClockIdentity and PTP domain number for any RTP/SDP clock-equivalence decision because RFC 7273 states that IEEE 1588 clocks are identified by a 64-bit ClockIdentity, that distinct PTP domains may have different grandmasters, and that senders and receivers should check both grandmaster ClockIdentity and PTP domain when PTP domains are signalled via SDP ([RFC 7273 Section 4.3](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)). ST Lab should also store whether clocks are signalled as traceable because RFC 7273 states that timestamps synchronized to traceable time sources can be directly compared even when synchronized to different sources or mechanisms, while traceable and non-traceable sources must not be mixed at a given signalling level ([RFC 7273 Sections 4.7 and 4.8](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).

### ST 2110 timing context

ST 2110-10:2022 is the ST 2110 system timing and definitions document according to the official SMPTE DOI page, but the fetched page does not expose timing clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022)). ST 2110-30:2025 secondary-hosted text states that PCM audio Media Clock and RTP Clock must comply with ST 2110-10 Clauses 7.3 and 7.4, and that RTP timestamps must comply with ST 2110-10 Clauses 7.5 and 7.7, but this must be verified against licensed SMPTE text before it is used as conformance evidence ([Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf), [SMPTE ST 2110-30:2025 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-30.2025)).

ST Lab should therefore separate three states for timing assertions: "RTP/RFC timing valid", "ST 2059 profile valid", and "ST 2110 timing valid", because RFC timing requirements are publicly clause-backed while ST 2059 and ST 2110 timing clauses were not publicly visible on the fetched official pages ([RFC 3550 Section 5.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html), [RFC 7273 Section 6](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html), [SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2021), [SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022)).

### Network device role model

ST Lab should model endpoint clocks, grandmaster candidates, boundary-clock-capable switches, transparent-clock-capable switches, ordinary switches, and non-PTP devices as distinct role/capability classes, because IEEE 1588-2019 states that it establishes timing topology, defines device behavior, supports profiles, and specifies network mappings, while secondary SMPTE guidance identifies overloaded or malfunctioning network devices as timing-quality risk sources ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html), [SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip)). The exact normative requirements for boundary-clock and transparent-clock behavior are Unverified in this report because the fetched IEEE 1588-2019 page does not expose the relevant clauses ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html)).

### Redundancy and resilience model

ST Lab should model multiple candidate PTP clocks, source diversity, failure detection, and clock-source switching explicitly because RFC 7273 allows multiple IEEE 1588 clocks to be listed at the same SDP level as candidate grandmasters or interchangeable reference-time sources, and it states that RTP senders and receivers may switch reference clock source while streaming for fault tolerance ([RFC 7273 Sections 4.3 and 6.1.2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)). Operational guidance from SMPTE identifies four PTP error conditions: interrupted event-message flow, wrong time information, inaccurate or deteriorated time quality, and overwhelming numbers of PTP messages, but the SMPTE page is an article and not normative standard text ([SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip)).

### Required outputs

ST Lab should output a PTP design report that lists the selected timing profile, grandmaster candidates, PTP domains, effective clock signalling per media flow, timing assumptions, unverified standards items, and validation evidence because RFC 7273 requires explicit interpretation of clock-source signalling and the official SMPTE/IEEE pages leave some profile-specific clauses inaccessible in public form ([RFC 7273 Sections 4.8, 5.4, and 6](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html), [SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2021), [IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html)). ST Lab should output warnings rather than hard failures when a rule is secondary operational guidance, because the WSTS/ATIS and Adeas/Xilinx documents are presentations or vendor whitepapers rather than normative standards ([WSTS/ATIS ST 2059-2 monitoring presentation](https://wsts.atis.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Monitoring-Analysis-of-SMPTE-ST-2059-2-PTP-Networks-Media-Devices.KERNEN.pdf), [Adeas/Xilinx ST 2059 whitepaper](https://www.adeas.nl/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Adeas_Xilinx-Whitepaper_ST2059-Time-Synchronisation.pdf)).

## Failure Modes and Risk Mitigations

| Failure mode                                                                | Evidence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         | Non-normative mitigation for ST Lab                                                                       |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Interrupted PTP event-message flow                                          | SMPTE article-level guidance lists interrupted PTP event-message flow in either direction or altogether as a PTP error condition ([SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   | Model bidirectional PTP reachability and flag missing reverse-path assumptions.                           |
| Wrong time source selected                                                  | SMPTE article-level guidance lists receiving wrong time information as a PTP error condition, and RFC 7273 warns that incorrect reference or media clock parameters may cause devices or streams to synchronize to unintended clock sources ([SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip), [RFC 7273 Section 7](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).                                                                                                     | Require source validation status for grandmasters and clock descriptions.                                 |
| Degraded timing quality due to overloaded or malfunctioning network devices | SMPTE article-level guidance identifies inaccurate or deteriorated time information, often due to overloaded network devices, as a PTP error condition ([SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Capture congestion risk, PTP-aware switch role, and monitoring telemetry requirements per path.           |
| PTP message overload or denial of service                                   | SMPTE article-level guidance lists overwhelming a device with high numbers of PTP messages as an error condition and denial-of-service risk ([SMPTE PTP broadcast article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/precision-time-protocol-for-synchronization-in-broadcast-over-ip)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | Track expected PTP message-rate assumptions and flag unknown or unbounded multicast/unicast behavior.     |
| Path asymmetry error                                                        | IEEE 1588-2019 official description states that the standard specifies how path-asymmetry corrections are made if asymmetry values are known, and the Adeas/Xilinx secondary whitepaper shows the delay calculation depends on an assumption that master-to-slave and slave-to-master network delays are equal ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html), [Adeas/Xilinx ST 2059 whitepaper](https://www.adeas.nl/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Adeas_Xilinx-Whitepaper_ST2059-Time-Synchronisation.pdf)). | Require an explicit `path_asymmetry_assumption` field and flag unknown asymmetry as a timing-risk item.   |
| Missing SDP clock signalling                                                | RFC 7273 states that missing media-clock signalling implies an asynchronous media clock and missing reference-clock signalling implies a local reference clock ([RFC 7273 Section 6](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Lower timing-confidence score when `ts-refclk` or `mediaclk` is absent.                                   |
| Mixed traceable and non-traceable clock sources                             | RFC 7273 states that traceable time sources must not be mixed with non-traceable time sources at any given level ([RFC 7273 Section 4.8](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          | Add deterministic validation failure for mixed traceability at one signalling level.                      |
| False confidence from endpoint-reported offset alone                        | WSTS/ATIS secondary guidance states that querying the offset of a slave reveals only so much and that asymmetries in time transfer and control-loop problems can remain undetected ([WSTS/ATIS ST 2059-2 monitoring presentation](https://wsts.atis.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Monitoring-Analysis-of-SMPTE-ST-2059-2-PTP-Networks-Media-Devices.KERNEN.pdf)).                                                                                                                                                                               | Require monitoring evidence source and distinguish device-self-reported offset from external measurement. |

## Open Questions / Unverified Items

- IEEE 1588-2019 detailed clause requirements for BMCA, clock classes, port states, boundary clocks, transparent clocks, profile conformance, management, and network mappings are Unverified in this report because the fetched official IEEE page provides scope and metadata but not full normative clauses ([IEEE 1588-2019 official page](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html)).
- SMPTE ST 2059-1 version/date and clause-level requirements are Unverified from official public source because the fetched SMPTE index lists the title but no date or clauses, while the 2015 date and epoch details were obtained from secondary guidance ([SMPTE Standards Index](https://www.smpte.org/standards/document-index/st), [Artel SMPTE Epoch article](https://www.artel.com/blog/aligning-to-the-smpte-epoch-for-synchronization/)).
- SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 normative profile parameters, message rates, domain defaults, BMCA constraints, mixed multicast/unicast requirements, and timing accuracy limits are Unverified because the official DOI page does not expose clauses ([SMPTE ST 2059-2:2021 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2021)).
- SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 timing clauses are Unverified because the official DOI page identifies System Timing and Definitions but does not expose timing clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022)).
- ST 2110-30:2025 references to ST 2110-10 Clauses 7.3, 7.4, 7.5, and 7.7 are secondary-hosted in this report and require verification against the licensed SMPTE publication ([Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf), [SMPTE ST 2110-30:2025 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-30.2025)).
- The commonly cited ST 2059-2 operational target of less than 1 microsecond within 5 seconds is treated as secondary guidance here because it was extracted from WSTS/ATIS and vendor material rather than an accessible official clause ([WSTS/ATIS ST 2059-2 monitoring presentation](https://wsts.atis.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Monitoring-Analysis-of-SMPTE-ST-2059-2-PTP-Networks-Media-Devices.KERNEN.pdf), [Adeas/Xilinx ST 2059 whitepaper](https://www.adeas.nl/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Adeas_Xilinx-Whitepaper_ST2059-Time-Synchronisation.pdf)).
- The current compatibility relationship between ST 2059-2:2021 and IEEE 1588-2019 is an open standards-process item in the December 2024 SMPTE report, which states that a revision is being developed to harmonize with IEEE 1588-2019 and notes issues around mixed unicast/multicast delay request signalling and TLV messages ([SMPTE Standards Quarterly Report December 2024](https://www.smpte.org/hubfs/SMPTE%20Standards%20Quarterly%20Report%20December%202024.pdf?hsLang=en)).
