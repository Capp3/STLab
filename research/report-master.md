# ST Lab ST 2110 Master Truth Package

## 1. Executive Summary

ST Lab should model SMPTE ST 2110 systems as four related but separable planes: essence/transport, timing, control, and management/monitoring. SMPTE‚Äôs public standards index identifies ST 2110-10 as ‚ÄúSystem Timing and Definitions,‚Äù ST 2110-20 as ‚ÄúUncompressed Active Video,‚Äù ST 2110-21 as ‚ÄúTraffic Shaping and Delivery Timing for Video,‚Äù ST 2110-22 as ‚ÄúConstant Bit-Rate Compressed Video,‚Äù ST 2110-30 as ‚ÄúProfessional Media Over Managed IP Networks: PCM Digital Audio,‚Äù ST 2110-31 as ‚ÄúAES3 Transparent Transport,‚Äù ST 2110-40 as ‚ÄúSMPTE ST 291-1 Ancillary Data,‚Äù and ST 2110-41 as ‚ÄúFast Metadata Framework‚Äù ([SMPTE Standards Index](https://www.smpte.org/standards/document-index/st)). The official DOI pages confirm active SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022, ST 2110-20:2022, ST 2110-21:2022, ST 2110-22:2022, ST 2110-30:2025, ST 2110-31, and ST 2110-40:2023 entries, but the accessible DOI content generally exposes only title/status metadata rather than full normative requirements ([SMPTE ST 2110-10 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-20 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-21 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-22 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-22.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-30 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-30.2025), [SMPTE ST 2110-31 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-31.2018), [SMPTE ST 2110-40 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023)).

The conservative rule for ST Lab is therefore: implement calculations and validation from openly verifiable primary or standards-track text, and mark SMPTE-internal clauses as ‚ÄúUnverified‚Äù unless the project has licensed standard text. RFC 4175 provides standards-track packetization details for uncompressed video over RTP, including active-sample-only payloads, pgroup constraints, per-packet RTP payload header structure, and SDP mapping; this can support a standards-derived bandwidth model for raw essence payload plus RTP/UDP/IP/Ethernet overhead, while ST 2110-20-specific constraints that are not visible in official public metadata remain Unverified ([RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)). RFC 3550 provides standards-track RTP/RTCP foundations, including sequence numbers, timestamps, RTCP monitoring, separate RTP sessions, and the absence of RTP-level QoS or delivery guarantees; ST Lab should not imply reliable delivery or capacity reservation merely because a stream is modeled as RTP ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550)).

Timing is a first-class design domain, not an annotation. IEEE 1588-2019 defines PTP for precise synchronization of clocks in packet-based networked systems, supports profiles, defines timing domains, establishes timing topology, and supports sub-microsecond synchronization with the possibility of sub-nanosecond transfer in properly designed networks ([IEEE 1588-2019](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html)). SMPTE ST 2059-2 is the SMPTE profile for use of IEEE 1588 PTP in professional broadcast applications, but public DOI/repository content does not expose the full profile parameter tables; any exact domain, message-rate, BMCA, or delay-mechanism rule must be treated as Unverified unless sourced from licensed ST 2059-2 text ([SMPTE ST 2059-2 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2015), [SMPTE ST 2059-2 GitHub repository](https://github.com/SMPTE/st2059-2)). SMPTE ER 1004:2021 is not normative, but it identifies timing as facility-critical, states that ST 2059-1 defines the SMPTE Epoch and signal alignment formulae, states that ST 2059-2 is a professional-broadcast PTP profile, and records risks including rogue leader, GNSS spoofing, delay/replay, management-message abuse, and PTP denial of service ([SMPTE ER 1004:2021](https://www.smpte.org/hubfs/er1004-2021.pdf?hsLang=en)).

NMOS should be modeled as the control-plane representation of discoverable resources and connection state, not as the media transport standard itself. AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3 defines discovery/registration resources including Nodes, Devices, Sources, Flows, Senders, and Receivers, and its data model states that Flows are internal content representations and not simply network streams ([AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/), [AMWA IS-04 Data Model v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/releases/v1.3.3/docs/Data_Model.html)). AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2 explicitly says it provides transport-independent connection management because ST 2110 does not specify how to connect Media Nodes, and its RTP behavior page defines transport parameters, SDP priority rules, SMPTE 2022-7 array-leg handling, and RTCP/FEC parameter sets for connection management ([AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/), [AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/releases/v1.1.2/docs/Behaviour_-_RTP_Transport_Type.html)).

## 2. Scope and Boundaries

This package covers design-time truth needed for ST Lab to plan ST 2110 systems: standards map, normative requirements, architecture model, data-model requirements, calculation requirements, risk register, and validation checklist. It covers ST 2110 transport and essences, PTP/timing standards and design requirements, engineering bandwidth/capacity calculations, and NMOS/control-plane standards and interactions, as requested. It does not claim to reproduce paywalled SMPTE normative text unless the statement is visible in public DOI metadata, a public SMPTE page, an IETF/IEEE/AMWA primary source, or an explicitly identified secondary copy.

Normative statements in this document are limited to statements directly supported by standards-track or official specification sources. RFC 2119-style requirements are quoted or paraphrased from RFCs and AMWA specifications where publicly accessible, while SMPTE internal ‚Äúshall‚Äù requirements are not reconstructed from memory. Where secondary-hosted SMPTE excerpts are used, this document labels the source as secondary and preserves uncertainty.

ST Lab should treat this package as a design-time synthesis, not as a substitute for licensed SMPTE/AES standards access. The official SMPTE DOI pages for most ST 2110 parts expose title/status metadata but not full normative clauses, so formal conformance and certification decisions require licensed standards review ([SMPTE ST 2110-10 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-21 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022), [SMPTE ST 2059-2 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2015)). This package is suitable for ST Lab‚Äôs planning-first model, calculation engine requirements, validation warnings, schematic/report outputs, and future simulation compatibility boundaries.

## 4. Cross-Domain Normative Requirements

### Essence and transport plane

- **RTP behavior**: ST Lab must model RTP as a real-time transport protocol that provides payload type identification, sequence numbering, timestamping, and delivery monitoring, but does not guarantee timely delivery, delivery, packet order, or QoS; this is a normative constraint on simulation and reporting language because RFC 3550 explicitly states these limitations ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550)).
- **RTP sessions**: ST Lab must model separate media streams as separate RTP sessions unless an encoding explicitly multiplexes them, because RFC 3550 states each medium is typically carried in a separate RTP session with its own RTCP packets and different destination transport address pairs ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550)).
- **Uncompressed video payload**: ST Lab must model uncompressed video payloads as active video samples only when using RFC 4175-derived calculations, because RFC 4175 states that only active samples are included in RTP payload and inactive samples plus horizontal and vertical blanking should not be transported ([RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **Pgroup integrity**: ST Lab must ensure RFC 4175-derived video packetization does not fragment within a pgroup, because RFC 4175 states video data must not be fragmented within a pgroup and each scan-line fragment length must be an integer multiple of pgroup size ([RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **Video timestamp grouping**: ST Lab must record that all RFC 4175 packets belonging to the same progressive frame have the same timestamp and all packets belonging to the same interlaced field have the same timestamp, because RFC 4175 states those requirements and prohibits packets from including data from multiple frames or fields ([RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **SDP mapping**: ST Lab must parse and generate SDP fields conservatively, because RFC 4566 defines the SDP line model, the order of required/optional fields, media/session override behavior, connection fields, media descriptions, and parser behavior for unknown attributes ([RFC 4566](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4566)).

### Timing plane

- **PTP base model**: ST Lab must model PTP as a protocol for synchronizing real-time clocks in networked distributed systems, with timing domains, timing topology, profiles, and mappings to UDP/IP and Ethernet, because IEEE 1588-2019 explicitly defines those scope areas ([IEEE 1588-2019](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html)).
- **Reference clock signaling**: ST Lab must model `a=ts-refclk` and `a=mediaclk` independently, because RFC 7273 defines timestamp reference clock and media clock source signaling and states a description should include both when available ([RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).
- **Absent clock attributes**: ST Lab must treat missing media clock signaling as asynchronous media clock and missing reference clock signaling as a local reference clock, because RFC 7273 explicitly states these assumptions ([RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).
- **Direct media clock dependency**: ST Lab must require reference-clock data when a direct-referenced media clock is signaled, because RFC 7273 states that when a direct-referenced media clock is signaled, reference clock signaling is required ([RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).
- **Clock equivalence**: ST Lab must compare both grandmaster ClockIdentity and PTP domain when determining PTP clock equivalence from SDP, because RFC 7273 says senders and receivers should check both when PTP domains are signaled via SDP ([RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).

### NMOS and control plane

- **IS-04 resource model**: ST Lab must model Nodes, Devices, Sources, Flows, Senders, and Receivers as separate resources, because AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3 lists those resources and its data-model page defines their relationships ([AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/), [AMWA IS-04 Data Model v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/releases/v1.3.3/docs/Data_Model.html)).
- **Flow is not network stream**: ST Lab must not equate an NMOS Flow one-to-one with a network stream, because AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3 states Flows do not simply represent network streams and can exist without being advertised via a Sender or streamed over a network ([AMWA IS-04 Data Model v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/releases/v1.3.3/docs/Data_Model.html)).
- **Connection management boundary**: ST Lab must model NMOS IS-05 as connection management rather than media definition, because AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2 states it provides a transport-independent way of connecting Media Nodes and that ST 2110 does not specify how to do this ([AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/)).
- **Transport parameter precedence**: ST Lab must implement IS-05‚Äôs rule that transport parameters in the same `PATCH` request take priority over contradictory SDP file data, because AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior states transport parameters take priority and must be used for activation when a contradiction occurs in the same request ([AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/releases/v1.1.2/docs/Behaviour_-_RTP_Transport_Type.html)).
- **Redundancy leg count**: ST Lab must validate IS-05 transport parameter arrays against constraints, because AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior states that when client requests include `transport_params`, they must have the same number of array elements, or legs, as specified in the constraints ([AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/releases/v1.1.2/docs/Behaviour_-_RTP_Transport_Type.html)).
- **Secure NMOS APIs**: ST Lab must distinguish secure-control-plane requirements from media-plane security, because AMWA BCP-003-01 forbids insecure HTTP within its scope, requires HTTPS and WSS behaviors, and explicitly states that securing ST 2110 streams is outside scope ([AMWA BCP-003-01 v1.0.0](https://specs.amwa.tv/bcp-003-01/releases/v1.0.0/docs/1.0._Secure_Communication.html)).

### Best practice, assumption, and unverified labels

- **Best practice**: ST Lab should surface PTP design warnings for rogue grandmaster, GNSS spoofing, replay, management-message storms, and PTP DoS because SMPTE ER 1004:2021 identifies these timing threat categories, but the engineering report is not a normative standard ([SMPTE ER 1004:2021](https://www.smpte.org/hubfs/er1004-2021.pdf?hsLang=en)).
- **Assumption-derived**: ST Lab may compute physical wire-rate capacity by summing payload, RTP, UDP, IP, Ethernet, VLAN, preamble, and inter-frame-gap overhead only if those overhead values are explicit user inputs or separately sourced; RFC 3550 and RFC 4175 provide RTP and payload-header facts, but Ethernet overhead rules are not defined by ST 2110 public metadata retrieved for this package ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550), [RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **Unverified**: Exact ST 2110-21 CMAX, VRX, packet read schedule, sender type, receiver type, and traffic-shaping values are Unverified in this package because the official public DOI page exposes the title/scope but not the normative tables or clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-21 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022)).

## 5. Architecture Model for ST Lab

### Essence/transport plane

ST Lab should model each essence stream as an independent transport object tied to its source Flow, Sender, RTP session, multicast/unicast addressing, packetization profile, and capacity impact. This separation is required by the combination of RTP‚Äôs separate-session model and NMOS‚Äôs explicit distinction between Flows and Senders, because RFC 3550 defines RTP sessions by destination transport address pairs and AMWA IS-04 states Senders/Receivers transmit and receive Flows over the network ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550), [AMWA IS-04 Data Model v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/releases/v1.3.3/docs/Data_Model.html)).

For uncompressed video, the transport model should include active width, active height, frame/field rate, sampling structure, bit depth, pgroup size, line/fragment count, payload header count, RTP timestamp rate, destination/source addressing, and SDP `rtpmap`/`fmtp` attributes. RFC 4175 defines active samples, pgroup packing, 2-octet extended sequence payload header, 6-octet per-line/partial-line header, and SDP mapping with `a=rtpmap` and `a=fmtp` ([RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).

For audio, ST Lab should support the conservative fact that ST 2110-30 is PCM Digital Audio and that a secondary-hosted copy states ST 2110-30:2025 specifies real-time RTP-based transport of PCM digital audio by reference to AES67, with SDP-based signaling and non-PCM audio outside ST 2110-30 scope. This source is secondary-hosted and therefore should be labeled as secondary in any compliance report, even though the official DOI confirms the active ST 2110-30:2025 entry ([SMPTE ST 2110-30 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-30.2025), [Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 excerpt](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf)).

### Timing plane

ST Lab should model timing as a graph of PTP domains, grandmaster candidates, boundary/transparent/ordinary clock assumptions, endpoint clock lock status, and per-stream timestamp-reference/media-clock declarations. IEEE 1588-2019 defines timing domains, timing topology, grandmaster synchronization, PTP profiles, and mappings to UDP/IP and Ethernet, while RFC 7273 defines SDP signaling for timestamp reference clocks and media clocks ([IEEE 1588-2019](https://standards.ieee.org/standard/1588-2019.html), [RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).

ST Lab should store SMPTE Epoch and ST 2059 profile status as explicit fields, not implicit defaults. SMPTE ER 1004:2021 states ST 2059-1 defines the SMPTE Epoch, signal alignment to the epoch, and formulae for ongoing alignment, and it states ST 2059-2 is a PTP profile for professional broadcast applications; because ER 1004 is an engineering report, not a normative standard, exact formulae and profile parameters remain Unverified without licensed ST 2059-1/ST 2059-2 text ([SMPTE ER 1004:2021](https://www.smpte.org/hubfs/er1004-2021.pdf?hsLang=en)).

### Control plane

ST Lab should model NMOS as a resource and connection state layer that references, but does not redefine, the essence transport. AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3 provides discovery/registration resources and AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2 provides connection management via `transport_params`, while IS-05 explicitly states ST 2110 does not specify this connection-management function ([AMWA IS-04 v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/), [AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/)).

The control-plane architecture should maintain both desired/staged and active connection state where IS-05 connection APIs expose those states. The top-level IS-05 page identifies immediate and delayed connections, single and bulk connections, and `transport_params`, while the IS-05 RTP behavior page defines transport parameter sets and activation behavior for RTP, SDP, RTCP, FEC, and SMPTE 2022-7 legs ([AMWA IS-05 v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/), [AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/releases/v1.1.2/docs/Behaviour_-_RTP_Transport_Type.html)).

### Management/monitoring context

ST Lab should model RTCP and NMOS security/monitoring separately from media transport capacity. RFC 3550 defines RTCP as the control protocol for monitoring data delivery, providing minimal control and identification functionality, while AMWA BCP-003-01 addresses secure HTTP/WebSocket API communications and explicitly excludes securing ST 2110 streams ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550), [AMWA BCP-003-01 v1.0.0](https://specs.amwa.tv/bcp-003-01/releases/v1.0.0/docs/1.0._Secure_Communication.html)).

ST Lab should maintain a separate risk context for PTP infrastructure because timing failure can disrupt picture, sound clarity, and facility coherence according to SMPTE ER 1004:2021. This is a best-practice risk-model requirement rather than a standards-conformance requirement because ER 1004:2021 is an engineering report and not a normative standard ([SMPTE ER 1004:2021](https://www.smpte.org/hubfs/er1004-2021.pdf?hsLang=en)).

## 9. Validation Checklist (design-time)

- **Standards inventory**: Confirm each modeled essence references an explicit standard part and version/date, and mark any exact SMPTE clause dependency as Unverified unless licensed standard text is present ([SMPTE Standards Index](https://www.smpte.org/standards/document-index/st)).
- **RTP session check**: Confirm each media stream has its own RTP session, destination transport address pair, SSRC context, and RTCP context unless a verified payload format permits multiplexing ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550)).
- **Uncompressed video payload check**: Confirm RFC 4175-derived video calculations use active samples only, respect pgroup size, do not fragment within pgroup, and account for RFC 4175 payload headers ([RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **SDP syntax check**: Confirm SDP descriptions obey RFC 4566 line order, required fields, session/media override behavior, `c=`/`m=` address/port semantics, and unknown attribute handling ([RFC 4566](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4566)).
- **Clock signaling check**: Confirm each stream‚Äôs SDP or model includes `ts-refclk` and `mediaclk` where required by design policy, and apply RFC 7273 defaults if either attribute is absent ([RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html)).
- **PTP compatibility check**: Confirm GMID, PTP version, domain, traceability, and lock status are compatible between senders and receivers, because RFC 7273 requires GMID/domain checking for PTP equivalence and IS-04 PTP schema exposes GMID/traceable/locked fields ([RFC 7273](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7273.html), [AMWA IS-04 PTP clock schema v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/releases/v1.3.3/APIs/schemas/with-refs/clock_ptp.html)).
- **PTP profile check**: Confirm SMPTE ST 2059-2 profile assumptions are explicitly sourced, because official public ST 2059-2 metadata confirms the profile role but not exact parameter requirements ([SMPTE ST 2059-2 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2059-2.2015)).
- **Bandwidth trace check**: Confirm every bandwidth result stores payload rate, packetization method, packet rate, RTP overhead, network overhead assumptions, redundancy multiplier/leg, and link utilization; RFC 3550 and RFC 4175 support RTP/video components but not all link-layer overhead assumptions ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550), [RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **ST 2110-21 check**: Confirm traffic-shaping compliance is either backed by licensed ST 2110-21 values or explicitly marked Unverified, because official public metadata identifies the standard but not its normative timing tables ([SMPTE ST 2110-21 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022)).
- **NMOS resource check**: Confirm Nodes, Devices, Sources, Flows, Senders, and Receivers are modeled as distinct resources and that Flow-to-Sender assumptions are traceable ([AMWA IS-04 Data Model v1.3.3](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-04/releases/v1.3.3/docs/Data_Model.html)).
- **IS-05 connection check**: Confirm transport parameter arrays match constraints, SDP conflicts are resolved using IS-05 precedence rules, and ST 2022-7 mode uses one or two legs exactly as modeled in constraints ([AMWA IS-05 RTP behavior v1.1.2](https://specs.amwa.tv/is-05/releases/v1.1.2/docs/Behaviour_-_RTP_Transport_Type.html)).
- **Source-filter check**: Confirm SSM streams include `a=source-filter` where required, and warn if absent because RFC 4570 says absent source-filter defaults to accepting all sources to the connection address ([RFC 4570](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4570)).
- **DUP/redundancy check**: Confirm redundant RTP streams are modeled with explicit SDP grouping or equivalent control-plane relationship, because RFC 7104 defines `DUP` grouping semantics for redundant streams ([RFC 7104](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc7104)).
- **NMOS security check**: Confirm API security settings distinguish HTTPS/WSS requirements under BCP-003-01 from media-plane security, because BCP-003-01 explicitly excludes securing ST 2110 streams ([AMWA BCP-003-01 v1.0.0](https://specs.amwa.tv/bcp-003-01/releases/v1.0.0/docs/1.0._Secure_Communication.html)).

- **Layer-2 overhead policy**: Ethernet/VLAN/FCS/preamble/inter-frame-gap accounting is assumption-derived in this package and must be selected by ST Lab‚Äôs engineering policy, because the sourced ST 2110/RTP pages do not define a universal wire-rate accounting method ([RFC 3550](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc3550), [RFC 4175](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4175)).
- **Compressed video capacity model**: ST 2110-22 CBR compressed video capacity should be modeled from declared compressed bit rate and packetization overhead, but exact ST 2110-22 constraints are Unverified from the official public DOI metadata alone ([SMPTE ST 2110-22 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-22.2022)).
- **Ancillary/fast metadata capacity model**: ST 2110-40 and ST 2110-41 packetization and capacity details remain Unverified beyond official titles/scope, so ST Lab should provide extensible metadata stream objects rather than fixed formulas until licensed clauses are reviewed ([SMPTE ST 2110-40 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023), [SMPTE ST 2110-41 article](https://www.smpte.org/blog/understanding-standards-smpte-st-2110-41)).
- **NMOS authorization**: AMWA BCP-003-01 explicitly states fine-grained client authorization is not in scope, so any authorization model in ST Lab is Unverified against BCP-003-01 and should be sourced from other NMOS security specifications or local policy ([AMWA BCP-003-01 v1.0.0](https://specs.amwa.tv/bcp-003-01/releases/v1.0.0/docs/1.0._Secure_Communication.html)).
