# ST Lab ST 2110 Transport and Essence Truth Report

## Executive Summary

This report is a conservative ST 2110 transport and essence truth package for ST Lab, limited to publicly verifiable source material and explicitly labeled uncertainty. The authoritative public SMPTE records identify the active/latest ST 2110 parts and titles but do not expose clause-level normative text for ST 2110-10:2022, ST 2110-20:2022, ST 2110-21:2022, ST 2110-22:2022, ST 2110-30:2025, ST 2110-31:2018, or ST 2110-40:2023 on the DOI pages fetched for this report ([SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-21:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-22:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-22.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-30:2025 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-30.2025), [SMPTE ST 2110-31:2018 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-31.2018), [SMPTE ST 2110-40:2023 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023)). Clause-level ST Lab modeling rules in this report therefore use public IETF payload and RTP standards where directly relevant, plus a clearly marked secondary-hosted copy of ST 2110-30:2025 for PCM audio requirements that must be verified against the official SMPTE text before certification or contractual conformance claims ([RFC 4175](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html), [RFC 3190](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3190.html), [RFC 8331](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html), [Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf)).

ST Lab should model each essence as a distinct RTP flow/session unless a payload standard explicitly defines another relationship, because RFC 3550 states that separate audio and video media should be carried in separate RTP sessions with separate destination transport addresses and should not be demultiplexed within one RTP session by payload type or SSRC ([RFC 3550 Section 5.2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html)). ST Lab should treat uncompressed-video packetization as pgroup-aligned line or line-fragment transport when the modeled payload is RFC 4175 `video/raw`, because RFC 4175 requires shared samples to travel as pixel groups, requires 10-bit or 12-bit samples to be combined into octet-aligned pixel groups, and requires active-line content not to be fragmented inside a pgroup ([RFC 4175 Section 3](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html)). ST Lab should treat ANC payloads as timed to a frame or field and not mixed across frames or fields when the modeled payload is RFC 8331 `video/smpte291`, because RFC 8331 forbids ANC data from multiple frames in one progressive RTP packet and forbids ANC data from multiple fields in one interlaced RTP packet ([RFC 8331 Section 2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)).

The main uncertainty is ST 2110-specific constraint detail for video traffic shaping, delivery timing, compressed video, AES3 transparent transport, and ANC constraints beyond public titles, because the fetched official SMPTE DOI pages show titles, active/latest status, and version years but not normative clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-21:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-22:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-22.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-31:2018 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-31.2018), [SMPTE ST 2110-40:2023 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023)). For ST Lab, those topics should be implemented as explicit conformance data fields that accept licensed-standard-derived values, rather than hard-coded from uncited recollection or vendor summaries.

## Essence and Flow Engineering Model

### Suite structure and transport/control boundary

The SMPTE standards index lists ST 2110-10 as System Timing and Definitions, ST 2110-20 as Uncompressed Active Video, ST 2110-21 as Traffic Shaping and Delivery Timing for Video, ST 2110-22 as Constant Bit-Rate Compressed Video, ST 2110-30 as PCM Digital Audio, ST 2110-31 as AES3 Transparent Transport, ST 2110-40 as SMPTE ST 291-1 Ancillary Data, and ST 2110-41 as Fast Metadata Framework ([SMPTE Standards Index](https://www.smpte.org/standards/document-index/st)). The fetched SMPTE DOI pages verify the active/latest status for ST 2110-10:2022, ST 2110-20:2022, ST 2110-21:2022, ST 2110-22:2022, ST 2110-30:2025, ST 2110-31:2018, and ST 2110-40:2023, but they do not expose normative clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-21:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-22:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-22.2022), [SMPTE ST 2110-30:2025 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-30.2025), [SMPTE ST 2110-31:2018 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-31.2018), [SMPTE ST 2110-40:2023 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023)).

ST Lab should separate media transport modeling from control-plane modeling as a data-model rule, because RFC 3550 defines RTP sessions and transport addresses for media streams while the fetched SMPTE DOI metadata for ST 2110 parts does not expose any control-plane clauses ([RFC 3550 Section 5.2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html), [SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022)). Any NMOS, SDP distribution, orchestration, or connection-management behavior should be represented as control/management context around transport flows rather than as transport-plane behavior unless a licensed ST 2110 clause or an AMWA specification is explicitly attached to the model; that is an ST Lab modeling assumption derived from the absence of visible SMPTE control-plane clauses in the fetched DOI pages and from RFC 3550's definition of RTP session mechanics ([RFC 3550 Section 5.2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html), [SMPTE ST 2110-10:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-10.2022)).

### Video essence model

For RFC 4175 uncompressed video payloads, the engineering model is active-sample RTP transport by scan line, line fragment, or multiple lines per RTP packet, with pgroup alignment as the basic packetization granularity ([RFC 4175 Sections 3 and 4.3](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html)). The model must include sampling, width, height, depth, colorimetry, interlace state, chroma-position, RTP clock rate, line number, field identifier, per-line offset, and pgroup size because RFC 4175 defines those payload and SDP elements for uncompressed video transport ([RFC 4175 Sections 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, and 7](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html)).

ST 2110-20-specific differences from or constraints on RFC 4175 are Unverified in this report because the official ST 2110-20:2022 DOI page did not expose normative clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022)). ST Lab should therefore store `st2110_20_profile`, `licensed_clause_source`, and `conformance_status` fields separately from the RFC 4175 payload fields, because otherwise a design tool could incorrectly conflate public RFC payload behavior with full ST 2110-20 conformance ([RFC 4175 Sections 3 and 7](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html), [SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022)).

### Audio essence model

For PCM audio under ST 2110-30:2025, the secondary-hosted text states that the standard specifies real-time RTP-based transport of PCM digital audio by reference to AES67, with SDP-based signaling for metadata needed to receive and interpret the stream ([Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF Clause 1](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf)). The same secondary-hosted text states that non-PCM digital audio, including compressed audio, is outside ST 2110-30 scope, so ST Lab should classify compressed audio separately from ST 2110-30 PCM audio pending licensed verification ([Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF Clause 1](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf)).

The PCM audio model should store sample rate, packet time, channel count, channel-order parameter, conformance level, RTP clock rate, and dependency on ST 2110-10 clock/timestamp clauses, because ST 2110-30:2025 Clauses 6.1, 6.2.2, and 7 state those requirements in the secondary-hosted text ([Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF Clauses 6.1, 6.2.2, and 7](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf)). For L20 and L24 RTP payloads, the model should additionally store signed two's-complement sample format and most-significant-bit-first contiguous packing because RFC 3190 defines those properties for 20-bit and 24-bit linear audio ([RFC 3190 Section 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3190.html)).

### ANC and data-related model

For ANC payloads using RFC 8331, ST Lab should model ANC as `video/smpte291` RTP payloads carrying SMPTE ST 291-1 ANC data, not as opaque side metadata, because RFC 8331 registers `video/smpte291` and defines an RTP payload for SMPTE ST 291-1 ancillary data ([RFC 8331 Sections 1, 3, and 3.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)). The ANC model must include frame or field association, RTP timestamp, marker-bit expectation, ANC_Count, F field, C flag, Line_Number, Horizontal_Offset, StreamNum/S bit, DID, SDID, Data_Count, User_Data_Words, Checksum_Word, optional DID_SDID filter, and optional VPID_Code because RFC 8331 defines those fields and signaling parameters ([RFC 8331 Sections 2.1, 3.1, and 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)).

ST 2110-40-specific constraints beyond RFC 8331 are Unverified in this report because the official ST 2110-40:2023 DOI page did not expose normative clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-40:2023 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023)). ST Lab should therefore treat RFC 8331 validation as ANC RTP-payload validation and reserve a separate ST 2110-40 conformance flag for licensed-source validation ([RFC 8331 Sections 2 and 3.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html), [SMPTE ST 2110-40:2023 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-40.2023)).

### Redundancy and reliability context

SMPTE ST 2022-7 is identified by the SMPTE DOI page as "Seamless Protection Switching of RTP Datagrams," but the fetched page did not expose normative clauses ([SMPTE ST 2022-7 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2022-7.2019)). ST Lab should therefore represent redundancy as a flow-leg relationship with source/destination addresses, path identifiers, and protection-association metadata, but any "shall" behavior for ST 2022-7 switching, buffer, or path matching remains Unverified without licensed ST 2022-7 text ([SMPTE ST 2022-7 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2022-7.2019)).

## ST Lab Modeling Guidance

### Node fields specific to ST 2110 transport

These fields are non-normative ST Lab design guidance derived from the cited payload and RTP requirements, not standalone standards requirements. A node should store supported RTP media classes, supported payload subtypes, RTP clock capabilities, ST 2110 part support claims, and source of each conformance claim because RFC 3550 separates RTP payload type and session behavior while the SMPTE DOI pages do not expose all part-specific clauses ([RFC 3550 Sections 5.1 and 5.2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html), [SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022)).

Recommended node fields:

- `node_id`, `device_id`, `interface_ids`, and `media_roles`, with media roles mapped to RTP sessions because RFC 3550 recommends separate RTP sessions for separate media ([RFC 3550 Section 5.2](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html)).
- `supported_st2110_parts`, `claimed_version`, `claim_source`, and `claim_verification_status`, because the fetched SMPTE DOI pages verify titles and active/latest status but not clause-level behavior ([SMPTE Standards Index](https://www.smpte.org/standards/document-index/st)).
- `supported_payload_formats`, including `video/raw`, `audio/L20`, `audio/L24`, and `video/smpte291` only where backed by SDP, device data, or a user-entered source, because RFC 4175, RFC 3190, and RFC 8331 define those payload names and mappings ([RFC 4175 Section 7](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html), [RFC 3190 Section 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3190.html), [RFC 8331 Sections 3 and 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)).
- `ptp_or_reference_clock_context`, `rtp_clock_rate_support`, and `timestamp_reference_policy`, because RFC 3550 requires RTP timestamps to derive from a monotonically and linearly incrementing clock and notes that cross-media sync requires reference-clock pairing rather than direct timestamp comparison ([RFC 3550 Section 5.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html)).

### Link and flow fields

These fields are non-normative ST Lab design guidance intended to preserve calculation traceability. A link should store source IP, destination IP, RTP destination port, RTCP destination port where modeled, multicast/unicast mode, MTU, VLAN/segment label, redundancy group, and path identifier because RFC 3550 defines RTP sessions by destination transport address and discusses RTP/RTCP transport-address pairs ([RFC 3550 Sections 3, 5.2, and 11](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html)).

Recommended flow fields:

- `flow_id`, `essence_type`, `rtp_session_id`, `source_address`, `destination_address`, `destination_port`, `payload_type`, `encoding_name`, `clock_rate`, and `ssrc_policy`, because RTP payload type, timestamp, sequence number, and SSRC are defined as RTP fixed-header fields ([RFC 3550 Section 5.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3550.html)).
- `sdp_source`, `sdp_rtpmap`, `sdp_fmtp`, and `payload_parameter_origin`, because RFC 4175 and RFC 8331 map payload subtype and parameters into SDP `m=`, `a=rtpmap`, and `a=fmtp` fields ([RFC 4175 Section 7](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html), [RFC 8331 Section 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)).
- `st2110_part_claim`, `rfc_payload_valid`, and `st2110_conformance_valid`, because public payload RFC validation does not prove ST 2110 part conformance where licensed clauses were not extracted ([RFC 4175 Section 3](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html), [SMPTE ST 2110-20:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-20.2022)).

### Video-specific fields

Video flow records should include `width`, `height`, `sampling`, `depth`, `colorimetry`, `interlace`, `top_field_first`, `chroma_position`, `pgroup_size`, `line_number_range`, `field_id`, `line_offset`, `marker_policy`, `mtu`, and `fill_bits_policy` because RFC 4175 defines these concepts for uncompressed-video RTP payloads and SDP signaling ([RFC 4175 Sections 3, 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, and 7](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc4175.html)). ST 2110-21 traffic-shaping fields such as sender class, timing model, and receiver buffer parameters are Unverified in this report and should be stored as licensed-standard-derived fields because the public ST 2110-21 DOI page did not expose clauses ([SMPTE ST 2110-21:2022 DOI](https://doi.org/10.5594/SMPTE.ST2110-21.2022)).

### Audio-specific fields

PCM audio flow records should include `sample_rate`, `packet_time`, `channel_count`, `rtp_clock_rate`, `channel_order_parameter`, `channel_order_convention`, `conformance_level`, and `undefined_channel_grouping` because ST 2110-30:2025 Clauses 6.1, 6.2.2, and 7 define those requirements in the secondary-hosted text ([Drastic-hosted ST 2110-30:2025 PDF Clauses 6.1, 6.2.2, and 7](https://drastic.tv/images/documentation/st2110-30-2025.pdf)). L20/L24 records should also store signed two's-complement sample representation and contiguous MSB-first packing because RFC 3190 defines that behavior for 20-bit and 24-bit linear RTP audio ([RFC 3190 Section 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3190.html)).

### ANC-specific fields

ANC flow records should include `associated_video_flow_id`, `progressive_or_interlaced`, `frame_or_field_timestamp`, `anc_count`, `f_field`, `c_flag`, `line_number`, `horizontal_offset`, `streamnum`, `did`, `sdid`, `data_count`, `checksum_word`, `did_sdid_filter`, and `vpid_code` because RFC 8331 defines those RTP payload header fields and optional media type parameters ([RFC 8331 Sections 2.1, 3.1, and 4](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)). A receiver capability record should include DID/SDID interpretation support and raster-location sensitivity because RFC 8331 states that receiver capabilities may vary by ANC data item and by whether location matters ([RFC 8331 Section 3.1](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8331.html)).
